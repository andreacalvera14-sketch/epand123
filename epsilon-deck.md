## 2. Reporting & QA — Turning data into action

| Cadence | Audience | Focus |
|---|---|---|
| **Weekly** | Brand / agency | Pacing, ROAS, CTR/CVR, top/bottom SKUs, 1–2 actions |
| **Monthly** | Client Lead + brand marketing | Trend, SoV, learnings, next month's plan |
| **QBR** | Senior stakeholders | Strategic story, incrementality, roadmap |

**QA checklist — every client deliverable**
1. Numbers reconcile: platform ↔ report ↔ invoice
2. Date range, currency, time zone correct
3. Pacing % matches days elapsed
4. Spelling, client name, logos, tone
5. Every chart has a **"so what?"**
6. Peer review — fresh eyes catch tired-eye mistakes

> ❌ *"CTR is 0.42%"* → ✅ *"CTR on Brand A sponsored products −18% WoW, driven by 3 SKUs going OOS. Recommend pausing those line items and reallocating £4k to top-performing Kids."*

## 3. Issue Triage & Escalation — worked example

**Scenario:** *Friday 4pm — "Sponsored products spend dropped 60% overnight"*

1. **Acknowledge in 15 min** — "Investigating, will revert by 5:30pm."
2. **Diagnose root cause** — platform · pacing/caps · bids · SKU feed · taxonomy
3. **Confirm with data**, not assumption
4. **Escalate to Client Lead** — options + recommendation
5. **Reply to client** — cause, plan, ETA
6. **Monday post-mortem** — add feed-health alert

> *"Root cause: 40% of eligible SKUs OOS via 2am feed sync failure. (A) Hold spend, fix feed, relaunch Mon — ~£8k underspend. (B) Reallocate to top 20 in-stock SKUs today — protects pacing. Recommend B + feed-health alert. Need decision by 5pm."*
